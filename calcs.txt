Two independent components
The index combines at most two gaps, each worth 0–100 points:

Component	What it measures
ΔFinancial	Are expenses paid proportionally to each person's salary?
ΔDomestic	Is household work split equally (50/50)?
Final score = 100 − average of active gaps. 100 is perfect, 0 is maximum inequality.

1. Financial gap (ΔFinancial)
Question: Does each person's share of expenses match their share of total income?


ΔFinancial = |woman's expense share − woman's salary share| × 100
Shared expenses are split 50/50 before calculating.

Example — Alice earns 2 000 €, Bob earns 3 000 €:

Expense	Amount	Paid by
Rent	1 200 €	Alice
Food	600 €	Shared
Internet	50 €	Bob
Alice's effective expenses: 1 200 + 600/2 = 1 500 €
Bob's effective expenses: 50 + 600/2 = 350 €
Total expenses: 1 850 €
Salary share of Alice: 2 000/5 000 = 40 %
Expense share of Alice: 1 500/1 850 = 81 %

→ ΔFinancial = |81 − 40| = 41 points (Alice pays far more than her income justifies)

2. Domestic gap (ΔDomestic)
Question: How far from a 50/50 split is the household work?

Weekly hours are first converted to monthly (× 52/12 ≈ 4.33).


ΔDomestic = |woman's share of total domestic hours − 50 %| × 100
Example — same couple:

Task	Alice (h/week)	Bob (h/week)
Cooking & cleaning	10	3
Childcare	5	2
Groceries	3	0
DIY / gardening	0	4
Total	18	9
Alice's share: 18/(18+9) = 66.7 %

→ ΔDomestic = |66.7 − 50| = 16.7 points (Alice does two thirds of the work)

3. Final score
Both components are active in this example (salaries + expenses + domestic data entered).


Score = 100 − (41 + 16.7) / 2 = 100 − 28.9 = 71.1 / 100
→ 🟡 "Légère inégalité, quelques ajustements possibles."

Perfect equality example
Equal salaries (2 000 € each)
All expenses shared 50/50
Same domestic hours for both
ΔFinancial = 0, ΔDomestic = 0 → Score = 100 / 100 ✅

Combined contribution (informational, does not affect the score)
This separate figure shows each person's net economic contribution to the household, adding the monetary value of domestic work (at 11.88 €/h, the French minimum wage):


Contribution = Salary + (domestic hours/month × 11.88) − expenses paid
Using the example above:

Alice: 2 000 + (78 h × 11.88) − 1 500 = 1 427 €
Bob: 3 000 + (39 h × 11.88) − 350 = 3 113 €
This metric reflects who contributes more overall, counting both money and unpaid labour.
